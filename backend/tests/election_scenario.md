## Capture 1: Election Announced

```
The University of Toronto officially announced the 2026 Governing Council winter
election today. Constituency I — full-time undergraduate students in Arts & Science
— has 35 candidates running for just 2 seats across all three campuses. Campaign
period runs February 2nd through the 20th, with voting February 10th to 20th. The
Chief Returning Officer confirmed that a geographic diversity rule is in effect —
both winners cannot be from the same college or campus. Campaign spending is now
under a universal cap. There's already tension — last year's election saw Albert Pan
win from UTM with 499 votes, and the losing candidate Areeb Naeem publicly accused
the governance structure of being "a broken system that needs PROPER LEADERSHIP."
The CRO said new safeguards are in place but wouldn't elaborate.
```

## Capture 2: I Enter the Race

```
I, Ericsson Cui, declared my candidacy for Governing Council this morning. I'm a
first-year Computer Science student at UTM running on a platform of UTM
representation, budget accountability, and expanded Work Study positions. I'm also
running for three other UTM governance bodies simultaneously — Campus Council,
Academic Affairs Committee, and Campus Affairs Committee — making me the only
candidate seeking all four positions. My campaign manager is Yahya Alabed, a fellow
CS student at UTM with 998 LinkedIn followers and a Civic Leadership Award from the
City of Mississauga. We've set up our digital campaign HQ using our tech skills.
Rumor is that I and last year's winner Areeb Naeem had a falling out over whether to
push for a formal investigation into how the Governing Council allocates its $3.62
billion budget. I chose to focus on UTM-specific advocacy, which some student
activists see as too narrow.
```

## Capture 3: Albert Pan and Ajaswi Regmi Announce

```
Two more major candidates entered the race today. Albert Pan, the incumbent GC
member from UTM, is campaigning on his experience — he's served on the Campus
Affairs Committee, been through four elections, and wants to expand student awards
funding and experiential learning opportunities. Ajaswi Regmi from Woodsworth
College is focused on academic transparency. She's promising a full audit of UofT's
grading structures within 30 days of taking office and wants to redirect resources
toward what "directly supports learning." Ajaswi dropped a bombshell at her
announcement — she claims she found that students are "playing darts blindfolded"
with unclear grading structures, no access to syllabi during enrollment, and
information overload that makes campus resources impossible to navigate. She hinted
that certain faculties received disproportionate support while others were starved.
Albert looked visibly uncomfortable, probably because the GC he sits on approved
those exact budget allocations last year.
```

## Capture 4: The Scandal Breaks

```
Major drama. The Medium published an investigation by Samuel Kamalendran — their
News Editor who has overseen 60+ articles on UofT policy — revealing that Albert
Pan's Erindale Biology Society received preferential funding through a committee
that Pan himself sat on as a GC member. A clear conflict of interest. Pan claims
he recused himself from the relevant votes, but Samuel has committee meeting minutes
showing Pan was present and spoke in favor of the allocation before it was approved.
Ajaswi is calling for Pan to withdraw from the race. I released a measured statement
saying "all candidates should be held to the highest standards of governance — after
all, budgets reveal priorities" without naming Pan directly. Areeb Naeem posted on
social media in ALL-CAPS calling the entire Governing Council "A BROKEN SYSTEM THAT
NEEDS PROPER LEADERSHIP" and demanding they "SPEND ON WHAT STUDENTS NEED AND AVOID
WASTEFUL SPENDING."
```

## Capture 5: The Debate Gets Heated

```
First candidates' debate in the Student Centre tonight. Seven showed up — myself,
Pan, Ajaswi, Titobi Oriola from the BSA, Wolfgang van Gulik the UTSU Board
Director, Eddy Paez the Loran Scholar, and Samuel Kamalendran moderating for The
Medium. Ajaswi opened by demanding Pan explain the funding scandal. Pan fired back
accusing her of leaking budget documents. Titobi tried to redirect to equity issues
but got drowned out. I stayed above the fray until Pan said I was "trying to buy
credibility with startup money." I shot back: "I raised $150,000 because I know
budgets reveal priorities." The crowd went wild. About 300 students attended.
Samuel live-tweeted the whole thing.
```

## Capture 6: Campaign Trail — Dirty Tricks

```
Pan held a rally on UTM's quad — 150 people, "Students First" t-shirts. Overnight,
someone plastered flyers reading "EXPERIENCE AT WHAT? APPROVING YOUR OWN CLUB'S
FUNDING?" Pan suspects Ajaswi but she denies it. I did a town hall where Eddy Paez
broke down talking about feeling invisible as a rural Manitoba student at UofT. It
went viral on TikTok. Yahya helped run the Q&A and got emotional too. Mahi Mukati
told me he's torn — likes Pan's experience but the ethics stuff bothers him.
Someone also hacked Wolfgang's campaign email and leaked his opposition research
on Pan. Wolfgang says it's "an attack on governance integrity."
```

## Capture 7: Endorsements and Betrayals

```
The Medium endorsed me. Samuel said my platform had the most measurable outcomes.
But three staff resigned in protest — they say Samuel and Yahya are friends from
UTM's International Education Centre, which Samuel never disclosed. He insists it
didn't affect his reporting. The Pre-Med Society endorsed Eddy Paez for student
safety. The Startup Society backs Pan despite the scandal. ASSU endorsed Ajaswi.
Areeb Naeem surprised everyone by endorsing Titobi Oriola, calling him "the only
candidate with REAL credentials" and citing his BGC National Youth of the Year
award and $40,000+ RBC scholarship.
```

## Capture 9: Coalition Chaos

```
Alliances shifting hourly. Titobi was negotiating to endorse Pan for a diversity
initiative, but after the recording he cut ties and is considering endorsing me.
Wolfgang and Ajaswi announced a "transparency coalition" — his UTSU connections
plus her Tri-Campus network. Pan is isolated; three volunteers quit. Areeb Naeem
```

## Capture 10: The Apology

```
Pan held a press conference at 8am. Hadn't slept. He admitted the recording was
real — "a serious error in judgment" regarding his dual role on the funding
committee and Biology Society. He apologized to Ajaswi, the student body, and his
team. He's staying in the race: "running away would be easy — I owe it to the
students who voted for me." His voice cracked talking about how the GC was supposed
to be about students, not protecting his clubs. Ajaswi nodded from the audience but
didn't speak to him. Mahi respects the apology but isn't changing his vote. Neev
Ramani, the UTSU Senator from UC, tweeted: "This is why we need fresh voices."
```

## Capture 11: Election Day

```
Voting opened — all online this year. Record engagement: over 2,500 votes by noon
across three campuses. I voted from the UTM library at 10am. A shouting match broke
out between Pan and Ajaswi supporters in a UTSG study hall. Areeb showed up at
St. George with a megaphone urging "NONE OF THE ABOVE." Security moved him away.
Eddy organized 30 UTSC freshmen to vote together on a video call. Samuel and Yahya
were spotted at UTM Starbucks together — the friendship was real. The CRO told
The Medium that Pan's investigation concludes next week regardless.
```

## Capture 12: Results Night

```
Results are in. I, Ericsson Cui, won with 520 votes — first place. The geographic
diversity rule gave the second seat to Eddy Paez from UTSC with 480 votes. Pan
came third at 390, down from 499 last year. Ajaswi fourth with 370 — strong but
split by UTSG rivals. Titobi got 200, Wolfgang 150, Samuel 130. I gave my speech
at UTM: "Budgets reveal priorities, and my priority is you." Pan congratulated me
but left without talking to press. Ajaswi said her transparency work continues
"whether or not I hold a title." Titobi hugged me on the video call.
```

## Capture 13: The Fallout

```
Two days post-election, the CRO found Pan violated conflict-of-interest rules in
the Biology Society funding but stopped short of formal discipline, citing his
apology and proper use of funds. Ajaswi called it "a slap on the wrist" and filed
a complaint with the University Secretariat. Samuel published a post-election
analysis in The Medium: first-year turnout up 55%, driven by Eddy's UTSC organizing
and my quad-candidacy at UTM. Areeb's write-in campaign got 38 votes. Ian Robert
earned 85 votes — highest ever for Innis — and the Bingwi Neyaashi Anishaabek
First Nation posted congratulations.
```
