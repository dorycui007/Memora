## Capture 1: Election Announced

```
The Student Union officially announced the 2026 presidential election today.
Nominations close March 1st, with voting on March 15th. The election committee
chair, Professor Tanaka, confirmed that all registered students are eligible to
run. Campaign spending is capped at $500 per candidate. There's already tension
— last year's election was marred by allegations of voter suppression and the
losing candidate, Derek Hsu, publicly accused the committee of bias. Tanaka
said new safeguards are in place but wouldn't elaborate.
```

## Capture 2: Aisha Enters the Race

```
Aisha Nakamura declared her candidacy for Student Union president this morning.
She's currently VP of Academic Affairs and is running on a platform of expanded
peer tutoring, a 24/7 mental health hotline, and better accessibility services.
Her campaign manager is Priya Sharma, a senior pre-med student. They've set up
their campaign HQ in the library study room 204. Rumor is that Aisha and last
year's candidate Derek Hsu had a falling out over whether to push for a formal
investigation into last year's election. Aisha chose not to, which some student
activists see as a betrayal.
```

## Capture 3: Carlos and Mei-Lin Announce

```
Two more candidates entered the race today. Carlos Rivera, founder of the Startup
Society, is campaigning on an innovation platform — he wants to create a $50,000
student startup fund and convert the engineering basement into a makerspace. Mei-Lin
Park, current student government treasurer, is focused on budget transparency. She's
promising a full audit within 30 days of taking office and wants to redirect $25,000
from admin overhead to student programs. Mei-Lin dropped a bombshell at her
announcement — she claims she found irregularities in how the Student Union has been
spending its $400,000 annual budget, and hinted that certain clubs received
disproportionate funding. Carlos looked visibly uncomfortable, probably because the
Startup Society got a $15,000 grant last semester.
```

## Capture 4: The Scandal Breaks

```
Major drama. The Campus Chronicle published an investigation by Tyler Okonkwo
revealing that the Startup Society's $15,000 grant was approved by a committee
that included Carlos Rivera himself — a clear conflict of interest. Carlos claims
he recused himself from the vote, but Tyler has meeting minutes showing Carlos
was present and spoke in favor of the grant before the vote. Mei-Lin is calling
for Carlos to withdraw from the race. Aisha released a measured statement saying
"all candidates should be held to the highest ethical standards" without naming
Carlos directly. Derek Hsu posted on social media calling the whole student
government "a corrupt boys' club" even though half the candidates are women.
```

## Capture 5: The Debate Gets Heated

```
Had the first presidential debate in Lecture Hall A tonight. All five candidates
showed up — Aisha, Carlos, Mei-Lin, plus Kwame Asante from the debate team and
Elena Volkov from the campus newspaper. It got ugly fast. Mei-Lin opened by
demanding Carlos explain the grant scandal. Carlos fired back accusing Mei-Lin
of leaking confidential budget documents to the Chronicle. Kwame tried to
redirect to diversity issues but got drowned out. Elena sided with Mei-Lin on
transparency but criticized her for "weaponizing" the budget data. Aisha stayed
above the fray until Carlos accused her of "staying silent to protect the
establishment." She shot back: "I don't need to tear others down to build
something better." The crowd went wild. About 400 students attended and
multiple shouting matches broke out in the audience. Tyler Okonkwo live-tweeted
the whole thing.
```

## Capture 6: Campaign Trail — Dirty Tricks

```
Busy day on campus. Carlos held a rally on the main quad — drew about 200 people
and handed out "Innovate or Stagnate" t-shirts. But someone plastered the quad
with flyers reading "INNOVATE YOUR WAY OUT OF A CONFLICT OF INTEREST" overnight.
Carlos's team suspects Mei-Lin's campaign but she denies involvement. Meanwhile
Aisha did a town hall in the library where Sophie Chen, a freshman, broke down
crying about her roommate's mental health crisis and the 6-week waitlist for
campus counseling. It went viral on TikTok. Priya was there helping run the Q&A
and got emotional too. Jordan Williams told me he attended both events and is
torn — he likes Carlos's innovation fund but the ethics stuff bothers him. Also
heard that someone hacked into Elena Volkov's campaign email and leaked her
opposition research files on Carlos. Elena is furious and says it's "an attack
on press freedom."
```

## Capture 7: Endorsements and Betrayals

```
The Campus Chronicle editorial board decided to endorse Aisha Nakamura. Tyler
told me it came down to Aisha vs Carlos vs Mei-Lin, and they went with Aisha
because her proposals had the most measurable outcomes. But here's the twist —
three Chronicle staff members resigned in protest, saying the endorsement was
influenced by the fact that Tyler and Priya are dating, which Tyler never
disclosed. Tyler insists it didn't affect his reporting. The Pre-Med Society
endorsed Aisha for her mental health platform. The Startup Society backs Carlos
despite the scandal. The Accounting Club endorsed Mei-Lin. Derek Hsu
endorsed Kwame Asante in a surprise move, calling him "the only honest candidate
left in this circus."
```

## Capture 8: The Secret Recording

```
Everything blew up tonight. Someone leaked a recording of Carlos Rivera at a
private Startup Society meeting saying "Mei-Lin's audit threat is a joke — we
already moved those line items to a different account code. She'll never find
them." Carlos claims the recording is edited and taken out of context. Mei-Lin
held an emergency press conference calling for his immediate disqualification.
The election committee held an emergency session — Professor Tanaka announced
they're launching a formal investigation but Carlos can remain on the ballot
pending the outcome. Aisha called for "due process" but privately Priya told
me Aisha's team is scrambling to distance themselves. Jordan Williams publicly
switched his support from Carlos to Aisha, posting "I believed in innovation,
not deception." Sophie Chen organized a protest of 50 freshmen outside the
Student Union demanding accountability.
```

## Capture 9: Coalition Chaos

```
The alliances are shifting hourly. Kwame Asante was in talks to drop out and
endorse Carlos in exchange for a diversity initiative commitment, but after the
recording leaked, Kwame publicly cut ties with Carlos and is now considering
endorsing Aisha instead. Elena Volkov and Mei-Lin announced a joint "transparency
coalition" and are sharing campaign resources. Carlos is increasingly isolated —
three of his campaign volunteers quit and the engineering department faculty
advisor to the Startup Society released a statement expressing "deep concern."
Meanwhile Derek Hsu is loving the chaos, posting daily commentary and gaining a
huge following. He started a petition to cancel the election entirely and
"start over with candidates who aren't compromised." It has 800 signatures.
```

## Capture 10: The Apology

```
Carlos Rivera held a press conference at 8am. He looked terrible — clearly hadn't
slept. He admitted that the recording was real and that he had made "a serious
error in judgment" regarding the budget line items. He apologized to Mei-Lin
directly, to the student body, and to his own campaign team. He said he's staying
in the race because "running away would be the easy thing." His voice cracked when
he talked about his immigrant parents working three jobs to send him to college
and how he'd let them down. Half the room was in tears. Mei-Lin was in the
audience and nodded but didn't speak to him afterward. Jordan Williams posted
that he respects the apology but isn't changing his vote back.
```

## Capture 11: Election Day

```
Election day. The tension is insane. Record turnout — 3,200 students voted across
four campus locations. Polls open 8am to 8pm. I voted at the engineering building
station around noon, 15 minute wait. Security had to be called to the humanities
building station after a shouting match between Carlos and Mei-Lin supporters.
Derek Hsu showed up with a bullhorn urging people to write in "None of the Above."
Campus police asked him to move 50 feet from the polling station. Sophie Chen
organized a group of 30 freshmen to walk to the polls together. Tyler and Priya
were spotted holding hands outside the student center — guess the dating rumors
were true. Professor Tanaka told the Chronicle the investigation into Carlos's
budget irregularities will conclude next week regardless of the election outcome.
```

## Capture 12: Results Night

```
The results are in and it's a bloodbath. Aisha Nakamura wins with 38% of the vote.
Carlos Rivera came second with 22% — down from early polls showing him at 30%.
Mei-Lin Park surged to third with 25%, nearly overtaking Carlos on the strength of
the transparency movement. Kwame Asante got 10% and Elena Volkov got 5%. Aisha
gave her victory speech at Student Union Hall, thanking Priya and her team. She
struck a conciliatory tone: "This election showed that our campus cares deeply
about integrity, innovation, and inclusion. I want to earn the trust of every
student who didn't vote for me." Carlos congratulated her but left quickly without
talking to press. Mei-Lin gave a gracious concession and said her audit work would
continue "whether or not I hold a title." Kwame hugged Aisha on stage. Elena was
already filing her next investigative piece.
```

## Capture 13: The Fallout

```
Two days after the election, the investigation results dropped. The election
committee found that Carlos Rivera did violate conflict-of-interest rules in the
Startup Society grant approval, but stopped short of recommending formal
disciplinary action, citing his public apology and the fact that the grant money
was properly spent on student programs. Mei-Lin called the ruling "a slap on the
wrist" and announced she's filing a formal complaint with the university
administration. Carlos released a statement accepting the findings and committing
to full restitution. Meanwhile, the Chronicle published Tyler's post-election
analysis revealing that first-year voter turnout was up 60% — largely driven by
Sophie Chen's grassroots organizing. Derek Hsu's "None of the Above" write-in
campaign got 47 votes.
```

## Capture 14: Building the Administration

```
Aisha announced her administration appointments today and it's surprisingly
inclusive. Carlos Rivera agreed to chair an Innovation Committee — the makerspace
and startup fund ideas will move forward but with a new oversight structure that
Mei-Lin helped design. Mei-Lin accepted the role of Budget Oversight Advisor with
full audit authority. Kwame will lead the new Diversity & Inclusion Council.
Elena declined a formal role but negotiated guaranteed funding for the Chronicle's
investigative unit. Sophie Chen was appointed First-Year Representative — the
youngest person to ever hold a Student Union position. Priya Sharma will serve
as Chief of Staff. Even Derek Hsu got a bone — Aisha invited him to join an
Election Reform Committee to address his concerns about the process. He accepted,
calling it "the first smart thing student government has done in years."
```

## Capture 15: New Beginnings

```
First week of Aisha's presidency. The tutoring expansion launched and Sophie is
already recruiting 40 new peer tutors. The mental health hotline soft-launched
with a pilot group — Priya is coordinating with the counseling center. Carlos
presented the makerspace renovation plans to the engineering faculty and got
unanimous approval. Mei-Lin published her preliminary budget findings and it's
actually not as bad as she feared — most spending was legitimate, just poorly
documented. Kwame held the inaugural D&I Council meeting with representatives
from 22 student organizations. Jordan Williams joined the Innovation Committee
as a student-at-large member. Tyler published a long-form piece about how the
scandal ultimately strengthened student democracy. Professor Tanaka emailed
Aisha saying he's "cautiously optimistic" about the new administration. For
the first time in weeks, campus feels hopeful instead of angry.
```
